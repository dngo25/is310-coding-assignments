**GETting Culture Across APIs**

**API of my choice & Why**
Riot Games: https://developer.riotgames.com/docs/lol#data-dragon_data-assets
DDragon: "https://ddragon.leagueoflegends.com/cdn/13.17.1/data/en_US/champion.json"

I utilized the Riot Games Development API Key in order to collect data on the free champion weekly rotation for League of Legends. I chose this API because I used to 
play League of Legends, and I loved the lore and character design as Riot Games isn't just known for their game, but League of Legends' world-building. 
I believe it would be interesting to connect champion data to Europeana's real-life history to bring a richer understanding to potential inspirations and the 
influence culture has on these characters' design. I used the champion-v3 API and /lol/platform/v3/champion-rotations in order to complete this data collection. The output included the champions (or characters) that players can use in their
matches without any purchase. This week's champion rotation included the characters: 'Twisted Fate', 'LeBlanc', 'Kayle', 'Zilean', 'Corki', 'Nocturne', 'Maokai', 
'Jarvan IV', 'Vayne', 'Nidalee', 'Yorick', "Kog'Maw", 'Shyvana', 'Volibear', 'Nautilus', 'Viego', 'Ekko', 'Aatrox', 'Xayah', 'Rell']. At first, the champions were 
listed as a line of numbers, so to connect the numbered list to their respective champion, I used the ddragon to attach a name to the champion number.

**Europeana collection**
I selected the champion "Aatrox" as my item because I used to play Aatrox, and knowing that he is inspired by mythological stories, I wanted to use Europeana to search
for some historical context that relates to Aatrox as a character. Initrally I use Europeanan to search for the item "Aatrox" but nothing showed up. I then searched the
term "fallen angel" as Aatrox, as a character, is inspired by demons. As a result, the output displays a large amount of data about real-life culture in various places such as 
Italy describes fallen angels in its stories. 

**Outcome**
- I've limited the Europeana outputs to only list the top 4 search results, including the title, year, and link
- There are 20 champions/characters listed with their respective assigned numbers
